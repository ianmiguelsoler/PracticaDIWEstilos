(Introduction) Musical aspects

(Summary) Progressive rock, also known as prog rock or prog, is a rock music subgenre that originated in the United Kingdom throughout the mid-to-late 1960s and 1970s. It developed from psychedelic rock and originated, similarly to art rock, as an attempt to give greater artistic weight and credibility to rock music.

  • Progressive rock saw a high level of popularity throughout the 1970s.

  • Especially in the middle of the decade.

  • Bands such as Jethro Tull, The Moody Blues, Pink Floyd, Yes, King Crimson, Genesis, and Emerson, Lake & Palmer (ELP) were the genre's most influential groups.

(Conclussion) Progressive rock has influenced genres such as krautrock and post-punk, and it has fused with other forms of rock music to create such sub-genres as neo-classical metal and progressive metal.
